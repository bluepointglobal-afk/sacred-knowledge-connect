1. Executive Summary

Readiness score: 68 (illustrative). Baseline readiness is strong enough to respond to client questionnaires with an explicit limitations section. The primary shortfall is incomplete measurement (emissions inventory + safety leading indicators) and formal governance (named owner + cadence).

1.1 Key gaps

  • Emissions baseline not documented (Scope 1/2 methodology + sources).
  • Supplier code of conduct absent; onboarding and compliance monitoring undefined.
  • H&S reporting inconsistent; leading indicators not standardized.
  • ESG oversight not assigned at executive level.

1.2 90-day objective

Publish a client-ready report with grounded KPIs, limitations, and a tracked improvement plan.

4. Governance

Governance disclosures cover roles, responsibilities, cadence, and oversight. The company should appoint an ESG owner and establish a quarterly oversight forum.

4.1 Current state

Sustainability topics are handled across functions without a formal executive owner.

4.2 Gaps

  • No board/executive oversight defined.
  • No documented policies register and versioning.

4.3 Plan

Assign executive owner, create ESG charter, monthly KPI review, quarterly steering committee.

5. Strategy

Strategy disclosures describe how sustainability issues affect the business model and how strategy responds.

5.1 Business model and sustainability link

Customer procurement increasingly requires measurable emissions and safety performance.

5.2 Opportunities

  • Preferred supplier status via credible disclosures.
  • Operational efficiency from energy/fuel tracking.

5.3 Limitations

  • No documented materiality assessment.
  • No scenario analysis.
