# UK Islamic Education Programs - Research
## For Nabawi Scholars Immersion Design Reference
## Date: 2026-02-03

## Target Programs to Model After

### 1. Al-Khoei Foundation (London)
- **Focus:** Islamic education for youth
- **Programs:** Weekend schools, summer camps, Quran circles
- **Age Range:** 7-17
- **Strong Points:** Established trust, mosque-based, community-driven

### 2. Islamic Shakhsiyah Foundation
- **Focus:** Character education through Islamic values
- **Programs:** Full-time Islamic schools across UK
- **Age Range:** 4-16
- **Strong Points:** Ofsted-rated "Good," holistic Islamic education

### 3. Ebrahim College (London)
- **Focus:** Islamic scholarship and Arabic studies
- **Programs:** Weekend courses, summer schools, international programs
- **Age Range:** Adults + youth programs
- **Strong Points:** High scholarly standards, partnerships with Islamic universities abroad

### 4. Markfield Institute of Higher Education
- **Focus:** Islamic studies at university level
- **Programs:** Degrees, diplomas, short courses
- **Age Range:** 18+
- **Strong Points:** Academic rigor, international recognition

## Key Design Elements to Incorporate

### Visual Identity
- **Color Palette:** Deep emerald green (Islamic heritage) + gold accents (excellence)
- **Typography:** Modern serif (trust) + clean sans-serif (accessibility)
- **Imagery:** Historical Madinah sites, student groups (modest, diverse), Arabic calligraphy elements

### Trust Signals
- Partnership logos: Madinah Islamic University, established UK Islamic schools
- Testimonials from parents + students (with photos, names, schools)
- Clear safety protocols visible upfront
- Ofsted-style "What Parents Say" section
- Video tour of accommodations in Madinah

### Program Structure (Model)
**3 Tiers:**
1. **Explorer (15 days):** Summer introduction program
2. **Immersion (30 days):** Full summer intensive
3. **Scholar Track (45 days):** Advanced with university credit pathway

**Daily Schedule Highlights:**
- Fajr: Haram prayer + spiritual reflection
- Morning: Arabic language (Madinah Islamic University)
- Midday: Islamic studies (scholars-led halaqat)
- Afternoon: Cultural immersion (historical sites, heritage)
- Evening: Group activities, peer mentorship, Quran circles

### Parent-Focused Content
- "Is this safe?" FAQ section (detailed safety protocols)
- "What do they learn?" Curriculum breakdown by week
- "How much does it cost?" Transparent pricing with payment plans
- "Who supervises them?" Staff bios with credentials + photos
- "What if my child gets homesick?" Support protocols

### Booking Journey (Friction-Free)
1. Landing → "Apply Now" (big, clear CTA)
2. Short form: Student name, age, school, parent contact
3. Automated email: "Thank you! Here's what happens next..."
4. Call scheduled within 48h to discuss program fit
5. If accepted: Deposit link + onboarding portal access
6. Portal: Packing list, visa guidance, parent WhatsApp group invite

## Competitive Differentiation

**What Others Do:**
- Islamic schools: Full-time education (not immersion programs)
- Summer camps: UK-based (not international)
- Umrah packages: Families only (not youth-specific)

**What Nabawi Scholars Does:**
- Age-focused (12-17 only, not mixed ages)
- Education-centered (not just spiritual tourism)
- End-to-end service (visa → homecoming, no stress for parents)
- University credit pathway (appeals to academic families)
- Gender-separated cohorts (appeals to conservative families)

## Pricing Benchmark

**Comparable Programs:**
- UK Islamic summer camps (7 days): £400-600
- Language immersion programs (Spain, 2 weeks): £1,200-1,800
- Umrah packages (10 days, families): £2,000-3,500 per person

**Nabawi Scholars Target:**
- 15 days: £1,800-2,200
- 30 days: £3,200-3,800
- 45 days: £4,500-5,500

*Includes: flights (UK → Jeddah), accommodation, all meals, transport, university tuition, activities, insurance, supervision*
