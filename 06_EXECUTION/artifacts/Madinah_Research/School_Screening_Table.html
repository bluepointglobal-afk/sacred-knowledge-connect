How to read “Likelihood of interest”

  • High: explicitly Islamic secondary provision, active enrichment/trips, larger student body, established admin contacts.
  • Medium: Islamic ethos but smaller/independent, or unclear travel/external-program appetite.
  • Low: not a secondary school / primarily adult training / under formation / limited direct fit.

Outreach strategy notes (practical)

  • Lead with safeguarding + learning outcomes: a 1-page program brief (supervision ratios, accommodation, permissions, insurance, itinerary, staff credentials).
  • Target the right role: Headteacher/Principal + Trips Coordinator + Head of Islamic Studies (or Pastoral). For larger networks (AIC/AIIC), approach central admin first and request secondary-campus referral.
  • Offer a tiered package: (A) 15-student pilot cohort, (B) 30–45 student cohort, (C) recurring annual slot. Provide pricing per student and scholarships.
  • Plan by school calendar: UK schools often decide trips months ahead; Australia’s term dates differ. Suggest windows (post-exams / end-of-term) and provide alternatives.
  • Local champions: ask each institution to nominate 1 staff champion + 1 parent rep for approvals and comms.
  • Europe specifics: France/Belgium/NL often require strong compliance documentation for minors traveling internationally; provide translated summaries (FR/NL) where possible.
